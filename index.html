A Culinary and Economic Journey

Cansi is not just a dish; it is a cultural cornerstone of the Western Visayas region, particularly in Bacolod City.

About Cansi: Origin and Culture

Cansi is a unique culinary innovation that blends the savory beef soup known as Bulalo with the sour broth of Sinigang. Its distinct, tangy taste comes from the use of the batuan fruit, a local souring agent native to Negros Island.

The presence of the batuan fruit is strong evidence that Cansi originated in the Negros region. It is not from the Panay islands, as many people from Iloilo have noted the difference in flavor compared to their own cooking. This unique combination and its reliance on a local ingredient have made Cansi a powerful symbol of Negrosanon's culinary identity and a point of pride for the region.

Community & Tradition

A dish that brings families and communities together, celebrated at gatherings and festivals.

Cansi is more than food; it's a shared experience that strengthens community bonds and passes on culinary traditions from one generation to the next.

Symbol of the Region

The use of the local "batuan" fruit makes Cansi a proud symbol of Western Visayan identity.

The distinct tangy flavor from the batuan fruit is a hallmark of Negrosanon's cuisine, making Cansi a unique representation of the region's produce and culinary creativity.

A Source of Comfort

Known for its rich, soothing broth, Cansi is the ultimate comfort food for many Negrosanon'ss.

Whether enjoyed on a rainy day or shared with loved ones, Cansi provides a sense of warmth and comfort that is deeply ingrained in the local culture.

Eron's Cansihan: A Business Report

Our Journey Since 2015

Eron's Cansi officially opened its doors on January 9, 2015, with a mission to serve the most authentic Cansi in Bacolod City. From its humble beginnings, the business has grown into a culinary landmark, driven by a commitment to quality and a deep passion for Negrosanon's cuisine.

2. Business Strategies & Financials

Net Revenue

The business generates approximately Php 1 Million in net revenue annually, reflecting its strong customer base and consistent demand.

Operational Strategy

A consistent daily supply of fresh meat is maintained from the Libertad and Burgos markets to ensure the highest quality. This strategy focuses on a fast turnover, as meat stored in the freezer loses its ideal taste.

Market Demand

The business experiences a notable surge in demand during rainy weather, with customers seeking the comforting, hot soup, leading to an on-demand business pattern.

Product Philosophy

The core philosophy is to use organic ingredients, minimize MSG, and cook with passion to bring out the natural, rich flavor of the Cansi.

Marketing Strategy

The primary advertising strategy is to entertain and attract, leveraging partnerships with food vloggers and influencers on social media platforms to expand reach and showcase authentic Negrosanon's cuisine.

Competitive Advantage

Eron’s Cansi stands out for its authentic recipe, batuan-based sourness, generous servings, and reputation as one of Bacolod’s most trusted Cansi houses.

Digital Presence

Active on Facebook, TikTok, and Instagram, Eron’s Cansi engages with customers through food videos, behind-the-scenes cooking content, and promotional campaigns.

Technology & Innovation

The restaurant is developing a microwaveable Cansi product, which will feature the meat and soup packaged separately to maintain freshness and quality.

Community & Cultural Impact

The restaurant supports local farmers, preserves Negrosanon's heritage, and serves as a culinary landmark that connects Bacolod’s culture with its people.

The Sacred Recipe

Ingredients

  • Beef Shank or Short Ribs
  • Batuan Fruit (or Tamarind as a substitute)
  • Lemongrass
  • Ginger
  • Green Chilis
  • Fish Sauce
  • Peppercorns
  • Salt to taste
  • Unripe Jackfruit
  • Annatto Seeds (Achuete)
  • Onions & Garlic

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the beef shank and bone marrow. Simmer for 3-4 hours or until the beef is tender. Skim off any fat and impurities that rise to the surface.
  2. Add the batuan fruit and lemongrass to the broth. Continue to simmer for 30 minutes, allowing the sour flavor to infuse the soup.
  3. In a separate pan, sauté the onions and garlic in annatto oil until fragrant.
  4. Add the sautéed aromatics and sliced unripe jackfruit to the broth. Season with fish sauce and add the red chili peppers.
  5. Simmer until the jackfruit is tender. Adjust the seasoning to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Challenges and Future Outlook

Overcoming Challenges

Eron's Cansihan, like many businesses, faces various challenges that require strategic solutions:

Supply Chain Fluctuations

Ensuring a consistent supply of batuan fruit. Solution: Partnering with local farmers and exploring alternative souring agents.

Market Competition

The market for Cansi is highly competitive. Solution: Differentiating the brand through unique recipes, exceptional service, and a strong online presence.

Customer Experience

Maintaining high standards of service. Solution: Investing in staff training, gathering feedback, and implementing quality control.

Future Outlook

Eron's Cansihan is open to innovation. Future plans include:

Expansion

Opening new branches to reach a wider audience and introduce the authentic taste of Cansi to more people.

Sustainability Initiatives

Partnering with local farmers and promoting sustainable practices to ensure the long-term availability of local ingredients.

Innovation

Developing new products like a bottled Cansi broth or a frozen Cansi pack for international markets.
