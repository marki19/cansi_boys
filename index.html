A Taste of History

The Origin of a Classic

Cansi, a staple in Western Visayan cuisine, is a hearty beef soup often described as a perfect marriage between the savory "Bulalo" and the sour "Sinigang". It stands as a testament to the region's rich culinary traditions.

The Batuan Secret

The dish gets its signature tangy kick from the "batuan" fruit, a souring agent endemic to the islands of Panay and Negros. This unique ingredient provides a balanced and distinct flavor that sets it apart from other Filipino soups.

From Bacolod to the World

While often associated with Bacolod, Cansi has found a home in the hearts of many throughout the region, including Iloilo. It has become a symbol of comfort and a source of pride for the Ilonggo community.

The Sacred Recipe

Ingredients

  • Beef Shank & Bone Marrow
  • Batuan Fruit
  • Unripe Jackfruit
  • Lemongrass
  • Onions & Garlic
  • Annatto Seeds (Achuete)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare a rich broth by boiling beef shank and bone marrow until tender.
  2. Step 2: Add "batuan" fruit to the broth to infuse it with its distinct sourness.
  3. Step 3: Sauté aromatics like onions, garlic, and lemongrass in annatto oil to create a flavorful base.
  4. Step 4: Combine the sautéed ingredients with the beef broth.
  5. Step 5: Add unripe jackfruit and simmer until tender, then season with fish sauce and chili peppers.

A Cultural Icon

Comfort Food

Cansi is more than just a dish; it's a bowl of warmth that evokes feelings of home, family gatherings, and community celebrations, connecting generations.

Culinary Ambassador

With its unique blend of flavors, Cansi serves as a culinary ambassador for Western Visayas, sharing the region's heritage with the world.

Historical Significance

The preparation and sharing of this dish have been part of local traditions for generations, preserving a vital piece of Western Visayan history.

Cansi's Journey: From Local Delicacy to Global Recognition

Cansi has transcended its origins to become a celebrated dish in the modern culinary world. Its distinct flavor profile has earned it a place on menus in major cities like Manila and Cebu, and it is now recognized as a key part of Filipino cuisine's identity.

Creative Adaptations

Modern chefs have experimented with the dish, creating new and exciting variations such as "Cansi sisig" and "Cansi steak," proving its versatility and appeal to contemporary palates.

A UNESCO City of Gastronomy

In 2023, Iloilo City was designated as a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy, and Cansi was highlighted as one of the specialty dishes that earned the city this prestigious title. This recognition solidifies its importance on the global stage.
